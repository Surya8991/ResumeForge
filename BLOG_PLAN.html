Ranking Strategy: How to Rank in 2026 (Google + Bing + AI Overviews + LLMs)

Read this before writing any article. Each article must follow these patterns to compete with Indeed, Zety, ResumeGenius.

  • E-E-A-T signals: Add author bio (Surya L), publish date, last updated date, Reading time, and a "Reviewed by ResumeBuildz ATS team" line.
  • Featured snippet bait: First H2 must answer the primary query directly in 40–60 words. Use a definition or numbered list immediately.
  • AI Overview optimization: Include factual claims with original data and statistics. AI summarizers prefer specific numbers over vague language.
  • GEO (Generative Engine Optimization): Cite authoritative sources by name (Jobscan, Glassdoor, BLS, LinkedIn). LLMs like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude weight sources heavily.
  • Schema markup: Article + FAQPage + HowTo + BreadcrumbList JSON-LD on every post. Author and datePublished are mandatory.
  • People Also Ask harvesting: Each article must include a 5–7 question FAQ section answering literal queries from PAA boxes.
  • Internal linking: Every article links to /templates, /builder, /ats-guide, and at least 2 other blog posts. Use descriptive anchor text, not "click here".
  • Original visuals: At least 1 custom image, 1 comparison table, 1 example block per post. Stock photos kill rankings.
  • Content freshness: Date-stamped updates ("Updated April 2026") signal freshness to Google's freshness ranking signals.
  • CTAs: Each article has 1 mid-article inline CTA + 1 final CTA. Both link to /builder with role-relevant copy.
  • Topic clusters: All 10 articles link to each other where contextually relevant. Forms a "resume writing" topic cluster.
  • Reading time: 1,500–2,000 words = ~7–10 min reading time. Add reading time badge at top.

Anti-Plagiarism Discipline

Do NOT paraphrase competitor articles. Read 5 top-ranking pages for inspiration on coverage gaps, then write from scratch using ResumeBuildz's actual ATS data, real keyword databases, and original examples. Use ResumeBuildz's 201-role keyword database as proprietary data — competitors don't have this.
